Best Arguments

There is no single stat that decides this. The strongest cases depend on what you value.

Michael Jordan case

  • Peak dominance: 10 scoring titles, 5 MVPs, 6 Finals MVPs, and the highest playoff PPG ever.
  • Finals record: 6 championships in 6 Finals appearances, never pushed to Game 7 in the Finals.
  • Two-way peak: 1988 MVP and DPOY, 9 All-Defensive First Team selections.
  • Era control: the Bulls became the defining dynasty of the 1990s around his scoring pressure.

LeBron James case

  • Longevity and volume: all-time regular-season scoring leader with elite play across 22 seasons and counting.
  • All-around engine: top-tier scoring combined with historically rare passing, rebounding, and positional versatility.
  • Playoff volume: more playoff games, points, rebounds, and assists than Michael Jordan.
  • Portability: titles and Finals MVPs with Miami, Cleveland, and Los Angeles.
